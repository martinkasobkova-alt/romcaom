✦ Who stands behind Beyond Limits

I'm Anuradha

Yoga mentor, spiritual guide, teacher's assistant, eternal student of life. This is my story — and the reason Beyond Limits exists.

My journey

A journey of resilience and transformation

I am Romana Ezrová, spiritual name Anuradha dasi. My life has been shaped by the complexities of gender identity, societal expectations, and the courage to face adversity head-on.

My exploration of spirituality, yoga, and self-awareness helped me find a balance beyond the material world. After deep transformation in an ashram and on retreats in India and Cambodia, I found my true calling: to help others overcome their inner struggles and guide them to a deeper understanding of themselves.

How I see the world

I perceive the world not only with my eyes but also with my heart. I have a deep desire to help others, bringing more love, peace, and understanding into their lives. My journey is not only about self-discovery, but also about a deep connection with God and the entire universe.

I have a passion for yoga, meditation, and working with children. It is important to stay in touch with others — to share, travel, dance, and create. At the same time, I deeply value solitude and quiet moments, which allow me to truly be present with myself.

I also recognize that I am an inspiration for the transgender community, using my story to empower and encourage others on their own journeys. My heart is open to all who seek their path.

"Love is not what you want, but who you are."

My spiritual path

My spiritual values are deeply rooted. My guide on this journey is Sadguru Paramahamsa Vishwananda — Hari Bool — and my deep connection with him, along with an inner longing to come closer to my higher self.

I work on myself not only through the external world but also through deep inner processes: from awareness of the ego, to mastering the senses, to self-reflection. Another beautiful part of my life is a love for nature and shamanic traditions. I have a deep bond with indigenous shamanic cultures and feel a sacred connection with the Earth — a source of strength and inspiration.

Another part of my life

Teacher's assistant — working with children

Five years ago I started working as a babysitter in Germany, where I lived. I loved working with children so much that I decided to study to become a Teacher's Assistant after returning to the Czech Republic. I started working in children's homes, preparing weekend programs focused mainly on emotional intelligence.

Today I work as a personal assistant for children with autism (ASD) and children with combined disabilities. Children are themselves — spontaneous, natural, without the barriers we adults sometimes carry. They bring me as much as I try to bring them.
